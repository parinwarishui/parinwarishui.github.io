## Background

I was born in Phuket, Thailand, a small island built for tourism, with amazing beaches, the historical old town, and the mountainous, beautiful resorts with a sea view, ...and not much else.

Although as a result of growing up in Phuket, I've always wanted to get out of this island to somewhere else. As I got to interact with many people from all over the world, whether as tourists or long-term residents living here, I have always seen people of many different backgrounds in class at school and also in tourism attractions. One thing I've always learned is this: this world is a really, really huge planet, and people would fly a thousand miles to travel to a new place.

Growing up, I have had the privilege to be able to travel abroad with my family to a few countries around, such as Singapore and Japan. Little did I know these trips would be fundamental to my growth as a person and shape my hobbies and interests.

## Singapore

I still remember the very first few moments of traveling to Singapore. I was extremely young and I didn't know much. However, what interested me the most was how we traveled. I took the MRT from my hotel, which is Lavender MRT station, to other places, and all I felt was, "Why don't we have one at home?" My parents probably said something along the lines of, "Oh, because Singapore is a small island city with a lot of people, Phuket is just a town." It's a bit ironic and probably a diss towards Singapore itself that one thing I remember the most about this country is its public transport system and not any tourist attractions.

Going back home, I probably took a deep dive through the internet, and that was the first moment I got down the rabbit hole and never came back.

## Japan

Another trip that I went abroad was to Japan, particularly Tokyo. When I was traveling there, I was so amazed that trains are such an integral part of their society. It's not only a form of traveling between places, but those train stations are also a destination in themselves. There are shopping districts, malls, recreational places, and parks built right next to those stations. The train stations over there are not only means to an end but also a central hub for the people in the area. It's kind of fascinating that trains have so much representation in the culture over there. Not only are people actually using it in real life, but it's also featured in a lot of things and is part of the popular culture there, such as in anime.

## Growing Up

Growing up, I noticed that a lot of my hobbies and interests in my life probably have something related to public transport, urban mobility, or smart cities. I realized that I'm not only interested in the train cars or the bus vehicles themselves, but also everything surrounding it, such as the built environment of stations, how they connect to the cities, and how people commute using them in general. I also really enjoy watching YouTube videos related to public transport in general, not just buses or LRT. That's what I realized: oh, this is really my main hobby and interest. This is me.

## Opinions

I think all cities deserve a proper public transport system of some kind for the people. I do believe in the freedom of movement, and I do believe that public transport shouldn't have profit as its metric. We do not expect highways or roads to generate profit from people driving cars on them, so why should we expect public transport to make money when its main purpose should be to move people between places?
